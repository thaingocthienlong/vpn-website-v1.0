Homepage Direction

Which tone should drive the redesign?

All three options keep the current homepage sections, but tighten the order and reduce visual clutter. The differences are tone, spacing, and how “public institution” vs “community-facing” the page feels.

Editorial institutional Swiss grid

A. Editorial Institutional

Calm, credible, and sharper. Beige paper surfaces, dark navy typography, cleaner grids, and stronger reading rhythm.

Best if: you want the institute to feel authoritative, mature, and less “template-like.”
Warm civic Community-first

B. Warm Civic

More welcoming and human. Warmer neutrals, softer edges, and a friendlier tone without becoming playful.

Best if: you want the homepage to feel approachable for learners, partners, and community organizations.
Modern blue glass Refined continuity

C. Modern Blue Glass

Keeps the current navy DNA, but trims the clutter and turns the page into a cleaner premium public-site experience.

Best if: you want continuity with the current site, but executed with much better restraint and spacing.
